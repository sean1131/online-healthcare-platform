What are the most common symptoms of a cold? 14 months

Hello Dr, I often feel unwell during the change of seasons. What symptoms should I look out for to know if I just have a common cold?

- Anonymous
Answer:
The most common symptoms of a cold include a runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, sneezing, mild fever, coughing, watery eyes, and sometimes a mild headache or body aches. These symptoms are generally mild and develop gradually.
How can I tell if I have the flu or just a regular cold? 14 months

Sometimes my symptoms seem worse than usual. How do I know if it's the flu and not just a cold?

- Anonymous
Answered
Answer:
Flu symptoms tend to be more sudden and severe than those of a cold. Common flu symptoms include a high fever, chills, muscle aches, fatigue, headache, dry cough, and sometimes vomiting or diarrhea (more common in children). Colds usually develop gradually and rarely cause high fever or severe fatigue.

When should I worry about a headache? 13 months

I get headaches from time to time, but sometimes they feel different. When is a headache a sign of something more serious?

- Anonymous
Answered
Answer:
You should seek medical attention if your headache is sudden and severe, if it comes with neck stiffness, fever, confusion, vision changes, weakness, or after a head injury. Persistent headaches that do not respond to usual treatment or interfere with daily life also warrant a doctor's visit.
What are the early symptoms of diabetes? 12 months

Hi Dr, I have a family history of diabetes. What symptoms should I watch for in myself?

- Anonymous
Answer:
Early symptoms of diabetes can include frequent urination, increased thirst, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, blurred vision, slow healing of wounds, and increased hunger. Some people may have no symptoms. If you are at risk, regular screening is important.
How do I know if my stomach pain is serious? 11 months

Sometimes I get stomach aches after eating, but when should I worry and see a doctor?

- Anonymous
Answer:
Seek medical attention if your stomach pain is severe, persistent, or associated with symptoms such as fever, vomiting blood, blood in stool, jaundice, chest pain, difficulty breathing, or unexplained weight loss. Sudden, sharp pain may indicate a medical emergency.
